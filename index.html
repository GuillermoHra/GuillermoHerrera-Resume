About Guillermo
Guillermo Herrera-Arcos is a Mexican technologist driven by how global health problems can be transformed with hard science and technology to make better people’s lives. He is currently a robotics engineer student at Tecnologico de Monterrey in Mexico, studying and designing hardware and software solutions in the biomechatronics and bionics fields. He is also an undergraduate research assistant at the National Robotics Laboratory at Tecnologico de Monterrey, an undergraduate research assistant at the non-invasive brain machine interface systems laboratory at the University of Houston, and a research collaborator at the Center for Extreme Bionics at MIT. His research has spanned into: 1) hardware development of wearable robots for rehabilitation and human augmentation like robotic exoskeletons, and 2) the understanding of biological systems like the human brain and the neuromuscular complex, through computational methods, with the purpose of developing better means to interface with and control the human body. His dream is to understand these systems to develop robots and therapeutics that rehabilitate and heal the human body.
Roles
